# Aircraft Paint Schemes & Liveries by Biome

Reference guide for historically-accurate paint schemes and camouflage patterns. Organize fighters by biome for gameplay variation.

## Biome Categories

### **TEMPERATE / EUROPEAN**
Primary theater: Western Europe, Channel coast, early North African operations
- **RAF Standard:** Dark Green + Dark Earth upper, Sky/Aluminum undersides
- **Luftwaffe Standard:** RLM 70/71 (Dark Green) splinter pattern with RLM 65 (Light Blue) undersides
- **USAAF Standard:** Olive Drab over Neutral Gray

**Key Aircraft:**
- Spitfire Mk I (early Battle of Britain colors)
- Bf 109E (Western Front 1940)
- P-51 Mustang (natural metal or olive drab)
- B-17 (Olive Drab upper, gray lower)

**References:**
- Spitfire A & B camo schemes: https://www.dingeraviation.net/spitfire/spitmark.htm
- Bf 109 colors: https://www.cybermodeler.com/aircraft/bf109/bf109e_profile01.shtml

---

### **DESERT / ARID** 
Primary theater: North Africa (Egypt, Libya, Tunisia), Mediterranean
- **RAF Desert:** Dark Earth + Middle Stone upper, Azure Blue undersides
- **Luftwaffe Desert:** Sand Yellow/Light Stone with Olive Green patches (faded tan)
- **USAAF Desert:** Light Tan (Sand) over white/light undersides

**Key Aircraft:**
- Spitfire Mk V/IX (sand & stone upper, azure blue lower)
- Bf 109F (sand yellow with olive patches)
- P-40 Warhawk (shark mouth variants)

**References:**
- RAF Desert Camouflage guide: https://forum.gmodelart.com/raf-desert-camouflage-1942-1945-the-complete-guide-to-north-africa-and-mediterranean-aircraft-colors/
- Spitfire desert colors: https://www.aerialcombat.co.uk/2017/01/desert-camouflage-spitfire-en152.html
- Paint colors: https://acrylicosvallejo.com/en/product/hobby/sets/air-war-en/raf-colors-desert-scheme-mto-1940-1945-71163/

---

### **ARCTIC / WINTER**
Primary theater: Soviet Union, high altitude
- **Soviet Winter:** White distemper base coat over original colors (RLM 76 light blue)
- **Luftwaffe Winter:** RLM 65 (light blue) base + white wash
- **RAF Winter:** White or light gray over temperate colors

**Key Aircraft:**
- MiG-3 (white distemper, eroded texture)
- Bf 109G-6 (winter scheme with white wash)
- Any temperate variant with white overpaint

**References:**
- Soviet winter camouflage: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:MiG-3_aircraft_in_winter_distemper_camouflage,_World_War_II.jpg
- Bf 109 winter paint: RLM 65 base + white wash (field-applied)

---

### **TROPICAL / JUNGLE**
Primary theater: Pacific, South East Asia, Burma
- **RAF Tropical:** Dark Green + Azure Blue (dark blue undersides for sky concealment in intense tropical light)
- **Japanese:** Tan/Yellow upper, light blue lower
- **USAAF Tropical:** Olive Drab retained + light undersides

**Key Aircraft:**
- Spitfire Mk VIII (tropical variant, Bf 109G-2 trop)
- Any fighter adapted for Pacific theater ops

**References:**
- RAF tropical schemes varied by theater
- IPMS paint guides for tropical variants

---

### **URBAN / INDUSTRIAL**
Primary theater: Late-war Germany (defense of homeland), industrial complexes
- **Luftwaffe Late War:** RLM 74/75 (dark grey-green) splinter over RLM 76 (light blue)
- **RAF Late War:** Dark Grey + Dark Green (Ocean Grey scheme)
- **USAAF Natural Metal:** Polished aluminum (adopted 1944+, visible from ground for identification)

**Key Aircraft:**
- Me 262 (grey with white speckled effect from poorly-applied primer)
- Bf 109K-4 (RLM 74/75 splinters)
- Later Spitfires (Ocean Grey over Medium Sea Grey)

**References:**
- Me 262 grey/white scheme: https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/b/b2/Messerschmitt_Me_262A_at_the_National_Museum_of_the_USAF_%28cropped%29.jpg
- Late-war Bf 109 colors: https://www.mech9.com/2016/10/tamiya-148-messerschmitt-bf-109-e-3.html

## Squadron & Unit Markings (Variants)

For additional visual variety within a biome, layer squadron-specific markings:

### RAF Squadron ID
- Spinner stripes or bands (different colors per group)
- Tail markings and fuselage codes
- Example: 357th FG used red-yellow-red spinner bands

### Luftwaffe Unit
- Geschwader emblem on fuselage
- Individual aircraft number (white numerals)
- Example: JG 51 "Eagle" emblem, Bf 109E-3

### USAAF Bomb Group
- Tail letter and fuselage identifier
- Nose art (famous bombers)
- Group color coding (tail stripe)
- Example: 31st FG had red/yellow checkers, diagonal stripes

### B-17 Nose Art
- Extensive variety: pin-up girls, cartoon characters, names
- Each bomber squadron had distinctive art
- Mission tallies (small bombs) for kills/missions

**References:**
- B-17 nose art gallery: https://www.cybermodeler.com/aircraft/b-17/b-17_gallery.shtml
- Nose art article: https://www.nationalww2museum.org/war/articles/decoding-wwii-plane-nose-art
- 357th Fighter Group markings: https://toflyandfight.com/357th-fighter-group-aircraft-markings/
